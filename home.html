Vapi is one of the largest industrial areas in Gujarat in terms of small-scale industries, dominated by the chemical industry. NH 48 bisects the city. The western part was the original location of the town, while the eastern part consists mainly of industry and newer residential areas.

GEOGRAPHY

NH 48 bisects the city. The western part was the original location of the town, while the eastern part consists mainly of industry and newer residential areas. Mumbai is roughly 180 km to the south, and Surat is about 120 km to the north. The Arabian Sea, at the delta of the Damanganga, is about 7 km to the west. The city has tropical weather and enjoys three distinct seasons: winter, summer and monsoon, with rainfall ranging from 100 inches to 120 inches per annum. The Dhobikhadi, Bhilkhadi, Kolak and Damanganga rivers flow through Vapi. Surrounding locations include Daman, Dadra and Nagar Haveli, Umargam, Sarigam, Bhilad, Udvada, Sanjan and Pardi. Vapi shares its border with the union territory of Dadra and Nagar Haveli and Daman and Diu and Maharashtra.
